History

Crepês were believed to have been invented in the French region of Brittany in the 13th century, although some sources believe that they were invented earlier. Local legend claims that crêpes were invented when a 13th century woman spilled buckwheat porridge onto a flat cooking stone. A holiday in France and Belgium exists called the day of crêpes, when crêpes were offered to the French Catholic pilgrims visiting Rome for Candlemas. They were brought to North America when France colonized a large portion of land including northeastern Canada, the American midwest, and Louisiana. Crêpes gained further popularity in the United states when a pastry chef named Emy Wada introduced a cake that consisted of around twenty crêpes stacked with pastry cream.

Description

Crêpes are delicate, thin pancakes. Traditionally, they are made of buckwheat flour and served with savory fillings, but sweet crêpes are more common and savory crêpes can be made with regular flour as well. They are usually served with a variety of sweet and savory fillings, such as fruit, Nutella, ham, cheese, or jam. Crêpes can be eaten for dessert or any meal of the day.

Ingredients

  • 2 large room temperature eggs
  • 1 1/4 cups room temperature whole milk
  • 120 grams all-purpose flour
  • 4 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted, cooled
  • 1/2 teaspoon cold unsalted butter
  • fillings/toppings of choice

Directions

  1. In a blender, blend eggs, milk, flour, granulated sugar, salt, and 3 tablespoons melted butter until smooth,
    about 1 minute. Cover and refrigerate at least 1 hour or up to 2 days.
  2. Preheat a 9" nonstick skillet over medium heat. Melt 1/2 teaspoon cold butter in a preheated pan.
    Reduce heat to medium-low. Briefly whisk batter, then pour 1/4 cup into center of pan,
    lifting pan off heat and slightly tilting in a circular motion to help batter spread into an even circle.
    Cook until top is set and bottom is golden brown, about 45 seconds. Flip and cook on second side until cooked through,
    about 45 seconds more.
  3. Using a spatula, fold crêpe into quarters. Transfer to plate. Repeat with remaining batter for a total of 8 crêpes.
  4. serve with desired fillings and toppings.
  5. Enjoy!
